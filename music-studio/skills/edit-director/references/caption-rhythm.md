# Caption rhythm

## When to caption

- Burned-in caption every 60–90 seconds. Keeps phone-with-sound-off
  viewers anchored.
- At the moment of any non-obvious decision: "the kick lands on the
  off-beat", "this is a polyrhythm".
- Over the final demo: caption sounds, not words. "[filter sweep]",
  "[bass enters]".

## When not to caption

- Every line. That's a transcript, not a caption — kills retention.
- During VO that's already plain. Captions exist to anchor the
  unmoored, not to repeat what's loud.
- Over reaction frames. The face is the caption.

## Caption craft

- 4–6 words per caption. Anything longer doesn't fit the visual frame.
- Sentence case for normal captions; ALL CAPS for emphasis only (and
  rarely).
- Place at the bottom-third of the frame; YouTube's UI sometimes covers
  the very bottom.
- Time on-screen: minimum 1.2 seconds, maximum 4 seconds.

## Sound-design captions for the final demo

The demo's caption rhythm is its own design problem. The captions should
narrate the sound for sound-off viewers without spoiling the surprise:

- `[demo loop starts]` — let the viewer focus on what's happening.
- `[bass enters]` — when a layer comes in.
- `[filter opens]` — when a sweep happens.
- `[break]`, `[drop]` — for structural moments.

Skip captioning during a sustained section so the music has room.
