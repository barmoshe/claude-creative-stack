# Good vs bad — calibration examples

Use these to calibrate output. Replace the bad examples with the worst takes that came out of the channel's first 5 episodes; replace the good ones with what actually shipped.

## Viewer promise

| Bad | Good | Why the good one wins |
|---|---|---|
| "I used Claude to build a sequencer" | "I built a sequencer that writes impossible drum patterns" | Sells the result, not the workflow. |
| "Building a music app with AI" | "I made a polyrhythmic groovebox you can play in your browser" | Concrete; specifies what the viewer gets. |
| "AI music tools tutorial" | "What if you could draw a drum loop? I built it" | Has a hook, has a payoff. |

## Title

| Bad | Good | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Cool Synth I Made With AI" | "This Browser Synth Shouldn't Sound This Fat" | Concrete claim, curiosity gap. |
| "Building a Sampler in JavaScript" | "I Built a Sampler in 200 Lines of JavaScript" | Specific number. |
| "Music App Demo" | "Generated a Techno Loop From a Pencil Drawing" | The result is the title. |

## Thumbnail angle

| Bad | Good | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Screenshot of the IDE with code | Close-up of the app's interface, 3-word headline | App readable at 350 px. |
| Generic AI-generated cover art | Hand-drawn arrow pointing at the wow moment in a screenshot | Authentic > polished. |
| "AI MUSIC TOOL" in big letters over a stock photo | The sequencer's grid mid-pattern, with one cell circled | One core idea. |

## Final demo

| Bad | Good | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "and then I play around with it" | "an 8-bar techno loop using all 4 polyrhythms simultaneously, ending on a sub drop" | Specific. The viewer can imagine it. |
| "show off the features" | "play three loops back-to-back, each demonstrating one feature, no talking over the music" | Lets the music speak. |

## Leave-for-episode-2

| Bad | Good | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Maybe we'll add MIDI later" | "MIDI export is episode 2. Effects chain is episode 3." | Specific and committed. |

## When in doubt

If the user's idea, when stated as a viewer promise, makes you mildly embarrassed because it sounds bold — that's right. If it sounds safe and explanatory, it's wrong.
