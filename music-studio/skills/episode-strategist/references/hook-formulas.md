# Hook formulas — first 5–10 seconds

The cold open is the most important real estate on the channel. It sells or kills. Five formulas, each pre-tested in the music-tech niche.

## Formula 1 — Sound first

Open with the final-demo loop. No words. Then the title card.

> 0:00 — 4 bars of the demo loop play.
> 0:05 — Title card overlays, audio ducks 6 dB.
> 0:08 — Voiceover: "I built this in a weekend."

Best when the demo is the surprise.

## Formula 2 — Question + bait

Open with a one-sentence question that the viewer wants the answer to.

> 0:00 — "What if you could sequence drums by drawing circles?"
> 0:03 — A weird visual answers the question instantly (a spinning circle, drum hits firing on its dots).
> 0:08 — Title card.

Best when the build's premise is the surprise.

## Formula 3 — Bug-as-feature

Open with the moment the bug became the song.

> 0:00 — A glitchy, half-broken beat plays.
> 0:03 — Voiceover: "This was supposed to be a drum machine. It became a glitch instrument."
> 0:08 — Title card.

Best when the bug is the most-listenable thing.

## Formula 4 — Before/after

Open with the boring expected version, cut to the weird version.

> 0:00 — A standard 4-on-the-floor kick. "Here's what most drum machines do."
> 0:03 — Cut to the build's output: a polyrhythmic, off-grid pattern.
> 0:06 — "Here's what mine does."
> 0:08 — Title card.

Best for builds where the contrast tells the story.

## Formula 5 — On-camera reaction

Open with the creator hearing the demo for the first time on camera.

> 0:00 — Demo loop plays. Camera holds on creator's face.
> 0:04 — Reaction (laugh, headshake).
> 0:06 — "Okay so I need to explain how this happened."
> 0:08 — Title card.

Best when the creator is on-camera and the reaction is real.

## Forbidden cold-open patterns

- A logo intro before any sound.
- The word "today" (filler — viewer doesn't care what day it is).
- "What's up everybody" or any greeting before the hook.
- Long shots of the IDE before the demo plays.
- Any cold open where the AI workflow is mentioned before the music.
