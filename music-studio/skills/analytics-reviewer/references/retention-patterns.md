# Retention patterns — what curve shapes mean

Every YouTube retention curve tells a story. These are the shapes that recur on a music-tech build channel; pattern-match before proposing experiments.

## Healthy shape

```
100% ───╮
        ╰─────────╮___________
                                ╰____
0%                                       end
```

- Steep drop in the first 5 seconds (people who weren't your target leave).
- Plateau through the build segments.
- Re-engagement at the demo (small bump, not a drop).
- Gentle taper at the outro.

**Action:** keep doing what you're doing.

## Cold-open cliff

```
100% ╮
      ╲
       ╲____
            ╰__________
0%                           end
```

The drop in the first 10 s exceeds the channel baseline by ≥ 5 percentage points.

**What it means:** the cold open didn't sell.

**Experiments to propose:**
- Test sound-first cold opens for the next 3 episodes.
- Re-evaluate the title-thumbnail-cold-open consistency. The viewer who clicked expected one thing; you delivered another.

## Mid-build slump

```
100% ───╮
         ╰_______
                  ╲____
                        ╰__
0%                            end
```

Plateau breaks into a noticeable second drop somewhere between 1:00 and 4:00.

**What it means:** the build narration is dragging.

**Experiments to propose:**
- Cut B-roll-only segments to under 8 seconds.
- Insert a captioned "wait, what?" moment every ~2 minutes.
- Pre-empt the slump: at the slump's timestamp, call back to the demo or to a future bug-as-feature beat.

## End-CTA cliff

```
100% ───────╮
             ╰___
                  ╲___╮
                        ╲╮
0%                         end
```

Steep drop at the outro CTA.

**What it means:** the demo ended (or didn't end satisfyingly), and viewers left during the CTA. This isn't necessarily bad — they got the thing they came for.

**Action:** only intervene if the drop happens BEFORE the demo ends.

## Demo-skip

```
100% ───╮________
                  ╰╮
                    ╲___
0%                       end
```

A clear drop right at the moment the demo starts.

**What it means:** the demo wasn't framed well. Viewers thought the video was over.

**Experiments to propose:**
- Caption the demo start with an explicit promise ("this is the part where I play through everything").
- Don't end build narration on a CTA-shaped sentence — that's a "I'm done" cue.

## Re-watches and chapter spikes

A localised spike in the curve (above 100%) means viewers scrubbed back. Common reasons:

- The demo loop is hooky → people listened twice. Good signal.
- A code segment was illegible → viewers paused/scrubbed. Bad signal — improve readability or replace with a caption.
- A bug moment was confusing → viewers tried to figure out what happened. Add a caption that explains.

## Threshold for "this is a real signal"

A curve deviation is meaningful if it's ≥ 5 percentage points off the channel baseline AND replicates in at least 2 episodes. One episode's weird curve might be noise.
