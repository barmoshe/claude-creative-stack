## 1. TL;DR

V1 is an Israeli short-vertical-video news app launched in beta on 1 May 2024 by **Keshet Digital**, the digital arm of Keshet Media Group (parent of the Keshet 12 broadcast channel) [(Hebrew Wikipedia "V1" — search snippet)](https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/V1) [(ice.co.il, Sep 2024 — search snippet)](https://www.ice.co.il/research/news/article/1031140). The product surface is the `v-1.co.il` web home and native iOS / Android apps published under "Keshet Broadcasting Ltd." (App Store id `6475302142`, Android bundle `com.keshet.v1`) [(App Store listing — search snippet)](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/v1-%D7%94%D7%97%D7%93%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%97%D7%93%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%AA/id6475302142) [(Google Play listing — search snippet)](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.keshet.v1). Reported reach by September 2024: ~900k downloads, ~800k active users, ~100k DAU [(ice.co.il — search snippet)](https://www.ice.co.il/research/news/article/1031140). Editor-in-chief: Netanel ("Neta") Livneh, former editor-in-chief of *Yedioth Ahronoth* [(walla brnz — search snippet)](https://b.walla.co.il/item/3833244).

## 3. Corporate structure

Best-guess public picture (each row separately cited):

- **Keshet Media Group** is a privately held Israeli media company founded 1993; CEO since 2002 is Avi Nir [(Keshet International "About" — search snippet)](https://www.keshetinternational.com/about-keshet-media-group). Search snippets summarising the English Wikipedia page state the controlling stake is held via **Central Bottling Company (CBC, the Israeli Coca-Cola bottler)** which owned ~64.6% of the group as of August 2024, with CBC itself controlled by the Wertheim family [(Wikipedia summary surfaced via search)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keshet_Media_Group). *(Confidence: medium — the Wikipedia page itself was unreachable in this environment; the ownership percentage is taken from the search-engine summary card.)*
- **Keshet 12** is the group's free-to-air commercial TV channel, launched 1 November 2017 as one of two replacements for the shared Channel 2 franchise (the other being Reshet 13) [(Channel 12 Wikipedia summary — search snippet)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Channel_12_(Israel)).
- **Hevrat HaHadashot / Israel Television News Company** produces the news for Keshet 12; it was a joint venture with Reshet until the 16 January 2019 merger after which "Keshet took full ownership" [(Hevrat HaHadashot Wikipedia summary — search snippet)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hevrat_HaHadashot).
- **Keshet International** is the group's global production/distribution arm, established 2012, with subsidiaries Keshet Studios (US), Keshet Productions (UK), Keshet Asia (Hong Kong) [(Wikipedia Keshet International — search snippet)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keshet_International).
- **mako** (`mako.co.il`) is described in public materials as "Keshet's digital arm" and one of the top three most-visited Israeli websites [(Keshet International "mako" page — search snippet)](https://www.keshetinternational.com/mako).
- **Keshet Digital** is the internal unit that *initiated and operates* the V1 app, headed (per public reporting) by CEO Uri Rozen, with Lior Yaniv as VP Content [(Hebrew Wikipedia "V1" — search snippet)](https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/V1) [(walla brnz — search snippet)](https://b.walla.co.il/item/3833244). Whether mako and Keshet Digital are the same legal unit, sibling units, or one inside the other is **not** unambiguously resolved in the public sources we could reach (see "What we don't know").

Sibling brands publicly associated with the Keshet family (cited only where a snippet supports them): **mako** (`mako.co.il`), **N12** (`n12.co.il`, the news brand sitting on Hevrat HaHadashot output), **Keshet 12** (broadcast channel), **Keshet International** (global format trade), **Keshet Studios / Productions / Asia** (international subsidiaries) [(Keshet International "About" — search snippet)](https://www.keshetinternational.com/about-keshet-media-group) [(Wikipedia summaries via search)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keshet_Media_Group).

## 8. Editorial / regulatory context

V1 sits inside the Keshet 12 family, which operates under Israel's commercial-broadcast regulatory frame:

- **Second Authority for Television and Radio (SATR / "הרשות השנייה")**: the public authority created by a 1990 Knesset law to regulate commercial broadcasting in Israel; it sets local-content quotas, examines schedules, and rules on fairness and truthfulness [(SATR overview, RIRM — search snippet)](https://www.rirm.org/en/satr-second-authority-for-television-and-radio-2/) [(Wikipedia "Second Israeli Broadcasting Authority" — search snippet)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Israeli_Broadcasting_Authority). Keshet 12 is one of the commercial channels under this frame.
- **Channel 2 → Keshet 12 / Reshet 13 split (1 November 2017)**: prior to that date, Keshet and Reshet were rotating concessionaires sharing the Channel 2 franchise; the regulator separated the franchises into two standalone channels [(Channel 12 Wikipedia summary — search snippet)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Channel_12_(Israel)).
- **News-production structure**: Hevrat HaHadashot / Israel Television News Company produces the news; Keshet took full ownership after the 16 January 2019 Reshet/Channel 10 merger [(Hevrat HaHadashot Wikipedia summary — search snippet)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hevrat_HaHadashot).
- **Israeli Press Council code of ethics** governs print/online journalism — accuracy, non-misleading headlines, conflict-of-interest rules, an ethics court that can sanction journalists or publications [(Press Councils Europe code text — search snippet)](https://www.presscouncils.eu/codes/28_il/) [(Accountable Journalism — search snippet)](https://accountablejournalism.org/ethics-codes/Israel-Rules). Whether V1 specifically subscribes to the Press Council code is **not** established in any source we reached.
- **Election-period speech rules and real-time security-incident protocols**: these exist in Israeli broadcast law and SATR practice [(SATR overview — search snippet)](https://www.rirm.org/en/satr-second-authority-for-television-and-radio-2/), but the public sources reached did not surface a V1-specific code statement. Treat election-period and security-incident handling as inherited-by-context rather than independently documented for V1.

## 10. What we don't know about V1 (gap log)

5. What is V1's audited (third-party-verified) DAU / MAU / time-on-app, distinct from the Keshet-supplied figures relayed in trade press?
6. What CMS, video pipeline, and analytics stack does V1 use internally?
7. Has V1 publicly stated whether it uses any AI-assisted production tooling for transcription, captioning, clip selection, or summarisation today?
8. Does V1 have a published editorial code of practice (Press Council membership, election-coverage rules, real-time security-incident protocols), and where is it published?
9. What is V1's accessibility posture — captioning languages, dark-mode roadmap, text-size controls, screen-reader support — beyond the forum-grade signal that the launch surface was light-only?
10. What is V1's brand book — locked palette hex/OKLCH values, typography stack, logo isolation rules, motion language? (None were directly inspectable.)
11. What audience demographics has Keshet publicly disclosed for V1 (age, geography, Hebrew-only vs Hebrew+Arabic+Russian+English speakers in Israel, diaspora reach)?
12. Is there a named partnership or contract between V1 and any external short-form-video distribution surface (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts) for cross-posting, given the parallel TikTok litigation posture?
